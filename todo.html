Ages: All, Price: Free entry, Name: PrideFest | Back in 1969, Stonewall was the first event in the region empowering the gay community to came out of its "collective closet". Since 1974, every summer, Seattle’s LGBT community honors the city’s first Gay Pride Week. With Seattle's LGBTQ+ community ranking at the 6th largest in the nation, it's no wonder many visitors come during PrideFest, which falls on the last Sunday of June. Every year there are around 300,000+ attendees throughout the week long event.

Ages: All, Price: Free entry but you can spend money on food for $~$$$, Place: Pike Place Market | First opened on the 17th of August in the year 1907, this 133 year old market is one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the United States. Seattle's iconic farmer's market overlooks the Elliot Bay waterfront bay and offers an array of food ranging from Chinese street food, to classic French cuisine. Stop by the Pike Place Fish Market and witness the most popular food stall in the market, serving locally caught salmon, crab, lobsters, shrimp and much more fresh seafood!

Ages: All, Price: Free entry, Place: Seattle Japanese Garden | Open to the public since June 1960, Seattle Japanese Garden is one of the most highly regarded Japanese-style gardens in North America. Tucked within a vast arboretum in a fast-growing city, the garden attracts 100,000+ visitors annually and is beloved both locally and by garden-lovers from over 30 countries around the world. Renowned landscape designer Juki Iida planned the Seattle Japanese Garden to be faithful to the principle of shizensa (the essence of nature).

Located 3 hours away from Seattle, this festival is located in The Gorge Ampitheater, situated right above the Columbia River Gorge. With a massive 20,000+ seat venue and a large-sitting terrace, this mind-blowing venue is one of the world's most scenic and elite natural views.
